We believe that by leveraging advanced sound technology, we can significantly improve the quality of life for individuals living in noisy areas.

The Harm of Noise Pollution

Noise pollution is often an overlooked aspect of environmental health, but its impact on human health is significant. Prolonged exposure to high levels of noise can lead to stress, sleep disturbances, and even cardiovascular issues. Despite the severe health implications, noise pollution is frequently ignored as a priority in urban planning and public health initiatives.

Beyond human health, noise pollution disrupts wildlife, affecting communication, navigation, and reproductive success among species. As urban areas expand, managing noise pollution becomes crucial to maintaining balanced ecosystems and a healthy environment for all living beings.

Decibel (dB) Sound Chart

The following table shows common sources of noise at various decibel levels and their potential impact on human health.

Sound Level (dB) Source Impact on Human Health
30 dB Whispering Minimal - generally safe, calming effect.
50 dB Moderate Rainfall Safe - comfortable for prolonged exposure.
70 dB Busy Traffic Annoyance, potential long-term hearing damage with prolonged exposure.
85 dB Heavy City Traffic, Lawnmower Hearing damage after 8 hours of exposure; use of hearing protection recommended.
100 dB Chainsaw, Car Horn (at close range) Hearing loss after 15 minutes of exposure; risk of permanent damage.
120 dB Rock Concert Pain threshold; immediate hearing damage with prolonged exposure.
140 dB Jet Engine (at close range) Severe pain; immediate risk of permanent hearing loss.

This chart illustrates how even common sounds can pose a risk to our hearing and health, especially if we are exposed to them daily or for extended periods. By raising awareness and promoting noise reduction measures, we can work towards a healthier and quieter environment.
